Churn Risk Signal Framework

A practitioner's guide to identifying, scoring, and responding to churn risk signals across the customer lifecycle

Sonya Freeney  |  Customer Success Manager  |  csplaybooks.com

⬇ Download as Word Document

Why early detection matters

By the time a customer formally announces their intent to leave, the decision has often already been made. The signals that predict churn typically appear weeks or months before an official notice arrives -- in usage data, in engagement patterns, in the emotional tone of support interactions.

This framework is built around five signal categories that, taken together, give a comprehensive picture of account health. No single signal is a definitive indicator on its own. It is the pattern and combination of signals that reveals true risk.

Each signal carries a weight from 1 to 4. A composite score is calculated as a percentage of the maximum possible score, which determines the risk tier.

Risk Tier Thresholds

Low Risk
0 - 19%
Continue standard cadence. Log signals and monitor monthly.
Moderate Risk
20 - 39%
Proactive outreach within 2 weeks. Schedule a health check call.
High Risk
40 - 64%
Recovery plan required. Involve leadership and build a success roadmap.
Critical Risk
65 - 100%
Immediate executive escalation. Emergency call within 48 hours.

Category 1: Engagement Signals

Engagement signals measure the quality and frequency of contact between the customer and your team. A sudden drop in responsiveness or a loss of internal champions are among the most reliable early warning signs of churn risk.

No executive sponsor contact in 90+ daysHigh (3)Act NowSchedule executive alignment call within 5 business days.
Primary champion left or changed rolesHigh (3)Act NowMap new stakeholders immediately. Book a relationship reset.
QBR / EBR declined or not scheduledMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksReframe as a 30-min informal check-in. Lower the barrier.
Response times to CSM outreach have slowedMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksSwitch channels. Try Loom, a direct call, or a different contact.
Drop in webinars, training, or user group attendanceLow (1)MonitorOffer a tailored 1:1 session on their specific workflow.

Category 2: Product Usage Signals

Usage data is often the most objective signal available to a CSM. When customers stop using a platform, they are effectively telling you something without saying a word. The key is distinguishing between seasonal dips and structural disengagement.
SignalRisk WeightUrgencyRecommended Action
Login / active user count declining 2+ consecutive monthsCritical (4)Act NowConduct a usage audit. Identify dropped-off users and schedule re-onboarding.
Core feature usage dropping or stalledHigh (3)Act NowRun a value audit tied to their original success metrics.
Integrations or automations disabled / disconnectedMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksReach out proactively. Offer a technical health check.
Spike in data exports (possible migration prep)Medium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksHave an honest conversation about satisfaction and evaluation.
No new users added despite known team growthLow (1)MonitorPresent a seat expansion plan tied to their team growth.

Category 3: Support and Sentiment Signals

How a customer talks about their experience -- and how they behave in support interactions -- reveals their underlying emotional state. Escalations, repeated complaints, and score drops are direct distress signals that require an immediate human response.
SignalRisk WeightUrgencyRecommended Action
Executive-level escalation or cancellation threatHigh (3)Act NowAssign exec sponsor. Emergency call within 48 hours. Draft remediation plan.
Unresolved critical tickets open 14+ daysHigh (3)Act NowEscalate to engineering. Send customer daily status updates.
NPS or CSAT dropped 15+ points from prior periodMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksHold a structured win-back conversation. Acknowledge and present improvement plan.
Repeated negative feedback across multiple channelsMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksConsolidate feedback and present it back. Co-create a resolution roadmap.
Ticket volume dropped sharply (possible disengagement)Low (1)MonitorProactively check on platform health -- do not assume satisfaction.

Category 4: Commercial and Relationship Signals

Commercial signals often indicate that a customer's organizational context has changed in ways that affect their commitment to your platform. Budget changes, M&A activity, and competitive evaluations are particularly high-stakes.
SignalRisk WeightUrgencyRecommended Action
Known competitive evaluation or pilot underwayCritical (4)Act NowRequest a meeting. Reinforce differentiated value tied to their use case.
Renewal stalled or major price concession being pushedHigh (3)Act NowInvolve leadership. Build a business case before discounting.
Overdue payment or pattern of late paymentsHigh (3)Act NowLoop in finance and leadership. Direct conversation about commitment.
Expansion / upsell conversations have gone quietMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksRevisit with new framing tied to a business outcome they care about.
Budget cuts, M&A activity, or major leadership changeMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksConduct a relationship reset. Retell your value story to new stakeholders.

Category 5: Outcome and Value Realization Signals

The most preventable form of churn is value gap churn -- where the customer never fully understood or experienced the ROI from your platform. This is where strong CSMs can have the most direct impact.
SignalRisk WeightUrgencyRecommended Action
Customer cannot articulate ROI or business outcomesCritical (4)Act NowBuild and deliver a custom ROI summary using their own data.
Onboarding success metrics unmet and not revisitedHigh (3)Act NowReset mutual goals. Create a 60-day gap-closure plan together.
Platform use narrowed significantly from original scopeMedium (2)Act Within 2 WeeksInvestigate root cause -- product gap, training gap, or business change?
No adoption of relevant new features or releasesLow (1)MonitorSend a personalized release summary showing their specific use cases.

Using This Framework in Your Practice

During QBRs and EBRs

Use the signal checklist as a pre-meeting diagnostic. Score the account before entering the room so you arrive with a clear picture of where you stand and which topics need direct attention.

In Your CRM

Map each signal category to a custom field or activity type in Salesforce or your CRM of choice. This allows you to track signal trends over time and surface patterns at the portfolio level.

At Onboarding Checkpoints

Run a lightweight version of this diagnostic at 30, 60, and 90 days post-launch. Early detection of value gap signals is far more effective than reactive intervention after churn momentum has built.
